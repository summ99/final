Mukda       Bangsong

'to eat'        'broadcast'
Thus, mukbang means, roughly, ‘a broadcast where people eat’.

A mukbang, also spelled meokbang, translates to 'eating show'. It is an online audiovisual broadcast in which a host consumes large quantities of food while interacting with the audience. It became popular in South Korea in 2010, and since then it has become a worldwide trend.

Why did people start watching and/or doing mukbangs?

Solomangarephobia - the fear of eating alone in public. More people in Korea are living alone at home.

Food Porn - The spread is always visually stimulating. ASMR videos appeal to those who enjoy hearing the texture and sounds during a meal.

Dieters - Satisfaction from food visuals and quantities. Some viewers suppress their appetites by watching others eat what they want.

Why are there issues with mukbangs?

Rewards - Mukbangers create experiences for the fulfillment of their communities and themselves. The audience is deeply engaged and 'thanks' the host by upvoting, sharing, or sending 'gifts' that can be redeemed for monetary payment on livestreaming platforms. Youtube mukbangs are paid according to views.

  • Promotion of overeating for the thrill of pleasure or fame
  • Encouragement of unhealthy eating behaviors(ie. "restrict, binge, repent, repeat”)
  • Perpetuate existing standards of unrealistic body images
  • Correlations to predatory behavior
